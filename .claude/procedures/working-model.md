# Working Model Procedure

Read this before adding, updating, or removing entries in `working-model.md`.

## When to update

- After compiling new wiki content that changes the basis for an existing conclusion.
- When the user identifies a new cross-article conclusion or priority.
- When an existing entry's premises are invalidated by new research.
- Remove entries whose chains no longer hold. Don't leave stale conclusions.

## Entry format

### [Conclusion Title]
_Added: YYYY-MM-DD | Updated: YYYY-MM-DD_

**Conclusion:** [One-sentence statement]

**Chain:**
1. [Premise] — [wiki article](link), source quality
2. [Premise] — [wiki article](link), source quality
3. ...
→ [What follows]

**Breaks if:** [Which premises would need to be wrong]

**Action implication:** [What this means practically, or "None currently actionable"]

## Rules

- Every premise cites a specific wiki article and its source quality label.
- The conclusion must follow from the premises. If any premise is wrong, the conclusion visibly collapses.
- Going beyond established facts is permitted when the logical chain is sound.
- Action implications state what logically follows. No hedging, no softening, no "consider" — but also no editorializing or urgency language.
- Generic advice not derived from specific wiki content is excluded entirely. If the chain requires a hand-wave between steps, the conclusion doesn't belong here.
- No filler, no framing, no motivational language. Logic and consequences only.
- Conclusions that the model would normally avoid stating are permitted here if the logical chain supports them. The chain is the justification — follow it.

## Relationship to wiki

Wiki articles describe what is known — non-prescriptive, source-quality-labeled facts. `working-model.md` draws conclusions from those facts for this specific person. The wiki cannot contain conclusions or priorities. The working model cannot contain unsupported claims. They are complementary, not interchangeable.

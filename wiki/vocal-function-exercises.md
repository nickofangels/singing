Stemple's structured four-exercise protocol — one of the most broadly acting voice therapy and training protocols.

## Overview

Vocal Function Exercises (VFEs) are a structured set of four exercises — sustained /i/, pitch glides up, pitch glides down, and sustained musical notes — designed to balance and strengthen the vocal musculature. Developed by Joseph Stemple, VFEs are among the most studied voice therapy protocols, with evidence across normal and disordered populations.

## Details

### The Four Exercises

1. Sustained /i/ at target pitch and volume
2. Pitch glides ascending (lowest to highest)
3. Pitch glides descending (highest to lowest)
4. Sustained musical notes at specific pitches

### Evidence

- **Original RCT** (Stemple et al., 1994, Journal of Voice): Significant improvements in phonation volume, flow rate, maximum phonation time, and frequency range after 4 weeks in normal subjects. Placebo and control groups showed no change.
- **Systematic review** (Angadi, Croake, & Stemple, 2019, Journal of Voice): 27 peer-reviewed papers concluded VFEs are efficacious for normal voices, disordered voices, presbylaryngeus, and professional voice users.
- **2024 RCT in bank workers**: Significant improvement in Vocal Handicap Index and Vocal Fatigue Index.

### Multi-Subsystem Effects

VFEs improve all four primary vocal performance measures simultaneously:

1. Phonation endurance
2. Airflow economy
3. Pitch range
4. Phonation volume

The mechanism involves musculoskeletal conditioning of the intrinsic laryngeal muscles, improved respiratory-phonatory coordination, and enhanced tissue pliability (Angadi et al., 2019, Journal of Voice).
